What we did

We ingested data from the Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC), USAspending.gov, and SAM.gov exclusions. Since these systems don't natively talk to each other, we built a pipeline to join them via Unique Entity ID (UEI).

Using 25 risk factors, we computed a Taxpayer Protection Score (0-100) for every entity and assigned a risk tier to prioritize oversight.

How to use this site

  • 1. Explore the overall landscape of risk and funding exposure.
  • 2. Drill down into specific entities to see why they scored low.
  • 3. Learn the definitions of complex audit findings.

Score Drivers

Taxpayer Protection Score = 100 - risk points.

  • Going Concern +25 pts
  • Material Weakness +20 pts
  • Repeat Finding +15 pts
  • Questioned Costs +15 pts
  • Significant Deficiency +10 pts
  • Low Risk Auditee Status -15 pts
  • Corrective Action Plan -5 pts

Plain Language Translator

Going Concern: Auditor doubts the entity can survive another year financially.
Material Weakness: A severe error in internal controls where financial data might be wrong.
Questioned Costs: Money spent without proper documentation or against the rules.
Repeat Finding: A problem identified in a previous audit that was not fixed.
This data helps focus questions, it is not proof of fraud.

Learn the Audit Terms

Going Concern

Substantial doubt about the entity's ability to continue operating.

Why it matters: High risk of bankruptcy.

Material Weakness

A deficiency in internal control over financial reporting.

Why it matters: Financials may be unreliable.

Questioned Costs

Costs that appear to be violation of provisions or lack documentation.

Why it matters: Potential misuse of funds.

Repeat Finding

A finding that was reported in the previous audit period.

Why it matters: Shows failure to fix problems.

Significant Deficiency

Less severe than material weakness, but important enough to report.

Why it matters: Warning sign of control issues.

Modified Opinion

Auditor concludes that financial statements are not free from error.

Why it matters: Data cannot be trusted fully.

Noncompliance

Failure to follow laws, regulations, or grant terms.

Why it matters: Legal and financial liability.

Corrective Action Plan

Document outlining how the auditee will fix the findings.

Why it matters: Shows intent to improve.

Low Risk Auditee

Entity that met specific criteria for clean audits in past years.

Why it matters: Generally safer investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Red tier mean?

Red tier indicates a Taxpayer Protection Score below 50. It means the entity has significant audit findings, such as material weaknesses or going concern issues, and high federal exposure. It warrants immediate oversight attention.

Does Red mean fraud?

No. A Red tier score indicates high risk and poor internal controls. It highlights where to look, but does not prove criminal intent or fraud on its own.

Why canâ€™t we match every SAM record?

SAM exclusion records often lack a Unique Entity ID (UEI). Without a common key, we cannot reliably join them to USAspending data without risking false positives.

What is UEI?

The Unique Entity ID (UEI) is a 12-character alphanumeric ID assigned to all entities (public and private companies, individuals, institutions, or organizations) who do business with the federal government.
